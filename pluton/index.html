What We Do at Sruti Kendra?

Sruti Kendra is a non-profit spiritual service organisation founded by Svami Moksha Vidyaananda Sarasvati in 2011,head quartered in Chennai.Sruti Kendra’s objective is transform society and help people lead empowered lives through the value system and knowledge embedded in ancient Indian wisdom symbolised by our Vedas and Scriptures.This transformation in our Society will help us to lead peaceful, happy and fulfilling lives in harmony with family, fellow beings and Nature. Another objective is to develop pride in India’s great tradition and help Nation building. All activities of Sruti Kendra are carried out in a socially inclusive manner.


Sruti Balakendra

Perhaps the most important wing of Sruti Kendra, Balakendra conducts one hour per week classes for children in residential areas (area Balakendras) as well as in schools (school Balakendras). These classes are conducted by well trained and motivated Sevaks (volunteers), which are free. The classes follow a pattern of stories depicting moral values, activities, games, group singing according to handbooks brought out by Sruti Kendra.  These classes have been adopted by schools as part of their regular curriculum also. Our format is to conduct training and orientation classes for the teachers of that school to handle the regular classes. This is carried out by our experienced Sevaks. Smart camps are conducted for children in any many areas and in schools in one, two and three day format which includes learning, art and craft, fun and games, festivals and heritage tours, which are of immense learning value. So far fifteen camps have been conducted which have been extremely well received. Children are given opportunities to show their talents during Swamiji’s yagna, festival celebrations, Guru Poornima functions and other such programmes. Environment Day rally was held in the Mogappair area, where 400 children participated and free tree saplings were distributed to all the children for developing in them loving care for Nature. Bala Sangamam is a programme conducted every year where children of all BalaKendras get-together for mass bhajans singing, competitions and presentations. The Balakendra movement is gaining strength day by day and at present reaches nearly 1200 children in Chennai and other districts in Tamil Nadu. Balakendra welcomes volunteers who have a passion in developing children, for handling area and school classes in Chennai and different areas of Tamil Nadu. Orientation programmes are conducted to induct new sevikas and for upgrading existing sevikas.

Sruti Yuvakendra

The vision of this wing is to make Youth change agents of our Society by making them internalise values, develop their independent thinking and improve communication skills, thereby developing their self-confidence. This is done through the theme of workshops, where our timeless values are given the context of contemporary events and personalities. There are videos, interactive sessions, group activities and discussions and presentations. The workshops are conducted by volunteers who have extensive experience in industry and business and teaching.

Sruti Sreekendra

Women are the pillars of our families and Society. They are powerful moulders of children’s character. They also influence the atmosphere and mindset of the family unit. Sree Kendra is a forum for home makers, where they learn Upanishads, Vedas, Bhagawad Gita and chanting. As a part of developing their inner self, they are also encouraged to handle Balakendra classes independently and also teach this knowledge to other home makers. They also have a bhajan group which gives public performances.

Sruti Shikshakendra

This study forum is handled by Swamiji, where the scriptures and other texts are taught and discussed for householders in general. Several Gnana yagnas are also conducted by Swamiji at various venues periodically, which are open to general public. The objective of these activities is to inculcate a discerning perspective to life and our role in Society.

Sruti Sevakendra

Sruti Kendra believes spirituality should be inclusive of the underprivileged sections of the Society also. The Balakendra classes are conducted in certain areas to benefit such children also. The annual children’s camps also has participants from underprivileged sections and orphanages. Our aim is to include these children in all the activities and projects of Sruti Kendra.  Seva Kendra also strive to contribute our might to help people in need in time of distress. Seva Kendra contributed 250 essentials kit to families affected by Chennai floods in December 2015

Sruti Sevakendra

Seminar and workshops for teachers are handled by Swamiji himself. There will be group discussion, queries and presentations. The focus is building a cogent relationship between values, attitudes governed by spirituality and building young minds.

Sruti Sevakendra

Parenting workshops are conducted by Swamiji. It includes presentation, with question and answer sessions to handle children in different difficult situations and stages of growth.

Swami Ji - Our Shining Beacon

"The ultimate aim of spirituality is to make a man realise that he is the indesctructible self, beyond the sea of changes", advocates Svami Moksha Vidhyaananda Sarasvati, who takes pride in the Indian culture and her rich resource of knowledge, the Sruti or the vedas. It was in 1992 that Swamiji fully immersed himself into the pursuit of spirituality. In December 2011, Swamiji formed Sruti Kendra with the main objective of reaching out to all sections of society and to spread the sacred spiritual knowledge, with special focus on children and the youth, spreading the glory of our rich culture. He inspires the householders also to walk the path of dynamic karma yoga to identify and reach the highest goals of life. An eloquent orator in Tamil, deep rooted in the Vedas, an erudite scholar, and ardent cricket fan, an inspiring and encouraging teacher, a humble disciple, an appreciator of the arts, a committed worker and overall, a good and simple human being, Svami Moksha Vidhyaananda Sarasvati is the quintessential modern day Swamiji and is perhaps the most interesting person one can meet.
